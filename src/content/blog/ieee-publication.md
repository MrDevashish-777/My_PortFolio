# Enhancing Accuracy for Multiple Object Detection

Publishing my research at the **IEEE REACS 2024** conference was a defining milestone in my journey as an AI engineer. My paper, titled *"Enhancing Accuracy for Multiple Object Detection"*, dives deep into the complexities of computer vision and proposes a novel hybrid model architecture designed to significantly reduce false positives in crowded visual environments.

## The Problem
Standard object detection models often struggle when environments become cluttered or when objects heavily occlude one another. While YOLO and SSD architectures offer incredible speed, their precision drops sharply in complex scenarios like dense traffic monitoring or crowded public spaces. 

## The Approach
My research focused on creating a hybrid pipeline that combines the raw speed of single-shot detectors with the nuanced contextual understanding of transformer-based attention mechanisms. 

Key milestones included:
- **Dataset Augmentation:** Curating and preprocessing over 50,000 highly occluded images.
- **Model Architecture:** Implementing a dual-stream neural network that processes both global contextual features and local bounding box regressions simultaneously.
- **Optimization:** Achieving a 14% increase in Mean Average Precision (mAP) compared to baseline models, without sacrificing real-time inference speeds.

## Looking Forward
Presenting this paper allowed me to engage with brilliant minds across the global AI community. The feedback I received has already inspired my next set of experiments involving real-time video stream analysis.
